Personal Information

I received my BS in Computer Science in 2013 from Isfahan University of Technology in Isfahan, Iran. I am currently studying PhD at Wayne State University. My research interests are Parallel Computing, GPGPU and Game design. Currently working on GOMC project which is an open-source Gibbs ensemble Monte Carlo simulation engine. In general I am interested in general-purpose computing on GPUs and optimization methods. Our objective is to use GPUs for different variety of computational problems.
Recently I have been working with department of pediatrics on an open source diffusion MRI analysis tool utilized with GPU. More information can be found on DSI Studio website and my github page.
My Curriculum Vitae and all of my information is available in PDF format. You can also find some of my projects and information about my research on this website.
